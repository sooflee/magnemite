A Plain-English Guide

Do watches actually make money?

Short answer: almost none do. But a few hold value — and a handful of cheap ones are worth more the second you buy them. Here's the whole thing, no jargon, no Rolex budget required.

The whole guide in 30 seconds

TL;DR

  • Almost all watches lose money. Outside three brands, the average watch resells for ~31% below what you paid.
  • Only Rolex, Patek Philippe & AP reliably hold value — and really only their steel sports models. If you can buy one at the store price, you basically win instantly.
  • The hack for the rest of us: a niche of cheap indie/micro watches sells out fast and instantly resells above retail. Some double.
  • The catch: fees and slow resale eat your profit. Treat it as a hobby that can hold value — not a stock.
01 — The Rules

Why most watches lose money (and which don't)

Whether a watch goes up comes down to a few simple things. Get these and you understand 90% of it:

What mattersThe ruleWhy
BrandOnly Rolex, Patek, AP hold valueThey make less than people want, on purpose
MaterialSteel beats goldMore people can afford steel, so demand is deeper. Gold dress watches drop 10–20% instantly
TypeSports beats dressDivers and chronographs are what everyone wants
Discontinued?Yes = price spikesSupply freezes, demand keeps going. The Patek 5711 tripled after it was killed
Scarce / waitlisted?Long wait = big premiumYou literally can't buy one at retail, so the resale price balloons

The one idea behind all of it: price goes up when supply is artificially smaller than demand. A long waitlist, a discontinued model, a 175-piece run, a lottery — all the same trick.

02 — If You Have $10k+

The blue-chip play

If you've got real money, the move is boring and reliable: get a steel sports Rolex/Patek/AP at the store (retail) price. The whole profit is the gap between that price and what it resells for. The hard part isn't money — it's getting the store to sell you one.

How the cheap ones work: the brand makes way fewer than people want and sells them by lottery or a drop that sells out in seconds. If you're in at retail, you profit. If you buy on resale, you're paying the premium, not earning it. For drop watches, flip fast — the hype fades.

04 — Reality Check

Don't fall for the hype

Most micro-brands actually lose money, and the internet wildly exaggerates the winners. Two things to internalize:

These trade below retail — buy them to wear, not to profit

Tudor (Black Bay), Grand Seiko, most affordable dive-watch micro-brands (Lorier, Serica, Nodus), Ressence, Czapek. All resell for 60–95% of retail. Nice watches; not investments.

"This watch 10x'd!" — usually fake

Most viral resale stories quote an asking price (what someone hopes to get) not a real sale. Examples that fell apart when checked:

  • Furlan Marri "~10×" — nope. Real sales are modest.
  • Autodromo Monoposto "4.5×" — that was one freak auction lot. Normal ones sell for less than retail ($375–500).
  • MoonSwatch / Halios / Unimatic "2×" — asking prices. Real sales sit near retail.

Rule: only trust a price someone actually paid (StockX last-sale, real auction results) — never a "for sale" listing.

05 — The Catch

What eats your profit

Even a winner isn't free money. Selling a watch costs 11–30% round-trip (dealer spreads, platform fees), and you might wait weeks or months to find a buyer — you can't sell a watch instantly like a stock. A watch that goes up 5% a year but costs you 3% a year to own and insure barely beats zero.

Honest take: the only watches that clearly beat the costs are (a) retail blue-chip Rolex/Patek/AP you can actually get, and (b) the cheap lottery/drop indies above — if you get them at retail. Everything else is a hobby you might break even on. This is info, not financial advice.

5 rules if you try this

  • Buy at retail, not resale. The profit is the gap between retail and resale. Pay resale and it's gone.
  • Only trust prices people actually paid. Ignore "for sale" listings — use StockX and auction results.
  • Scarcity beats hype. A lottery or a 175-piece cap lasts; generic buzz fades.
  • Drop watches: flip fast. The premium is biggest in the first weeks.
  • Keep the box & papers, don't polish it. Both quietly cost you 15–50% if missing/done.
